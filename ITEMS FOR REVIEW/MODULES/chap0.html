Getting the Most Out of Online Learning

Welcome to MXB107 2022, it’s great to have so many students interested in Statistics and Statistical Modelling, and hopefully, you all will have an exciting and rewarding semester. Online learning presents new challenges for both students and staff. The lack of face-to-face in-person communication between students and teachers means that both students and teachers need to adapt how they communicate and how they work to ensure that students learn the material. There are two broad areas where the differences between traditional in-person learning and online learning create challenges for students: Communication and Time and Task Management. To help MXB107 students navigate these challenges, here are some guidelines for communication and a suggested framework for managing learning activities.

As an online unit, MXB107 2022 will differ from the traditional classroom experience, and while some aspects of the unit may feel more isolated, there will be ample opportunity to interact with your fellow students and the teaching staff.

Weekly Readings

Instead of a traditional two-hour lecture each week, written notes or Weekly Readings will be released on Mondays by noon. These notes will be in the form of an electronic document with embedded videos.
Please note that the readings may be revised as needed throughout the semester.

You must read the weekly readings and watch the videos actively. Take notes of where you need more detail or explanation, and try to solve the example problems yourself. Most importantly, feel free to post your questions or comments about the weeks readings and videos via Slack.

Communications

In addition to email, MXB107 also has an active Slack workspace that you can join here https://join.slack.com/t/mxb1072022/shared_invite/zt-1cla55xlx-TnfY6YrcgEXWI2p025VYJQ. Students are encouraged to join this workspace and take full advantage of this platform as a way of both communicating with the teaching staff, but also with fellow students. The Slack workspace has five dedicated channels where you can post questions regarding the weekly readings, videos, assessments, workshops, and both general and random questions about MXB107.

The most effective way to communicate with the teaching staff and fellow students depends on the situation.

  • General questions about the readings, videos, workshops, assessment, or general (or random) questions or comments about the unit should be posted to the appropriate channel.

  • Posting questions to the appropriate channel ensures that all students can benefit from the response. If you have a question or comment, other people probably have the same questions or comments. Posting these on channels can help your fellow students learn and help the teaching staff create better resources or experiences so that everyone can get the most out of MXB107.

  • Email the teaching staff directly for questions about your circumstances or academic performance. For example, questions about how your assessment was marked, or other circumstances you wish to discuss privately with the teaching staff should be communicated via email.

Please note that responses to emails will be as timely as possible, but responses to questions posted publicly may not be immediate. Also, please note that teaching staff may have specific times allocated for responding to students’ publicly posted questions, so that may result in a natural delay in replying to your post. This delay in reply allows you and your fellow students to discuss problems and possible answers.

We request you maintain a respectful tone in any communication. It is a good idea to address the unit teaching staff and fellow students by their preferred manner and use clear and precise language. Please note that the teaching staff may work across multiple units, so your emails should include your student id number and the unit code.

Managing Your online Learning Experience

Learning online can seem overwhelming; without a regular schedule for attending classes and workshops, you can feel disoriented and confused about what you should be doing each week and when. In creating this the online version of MXB107, we have tried to address this by creating a general framework for your activities throughout the semester and some specific day-to-day schedules for your learning activities.

While each week’s specific activities may vary, in general, each week you should:

  • Read the posted Readings —

    • Each week, the Readings will be updated with the new notes and content for that week. These readings are the primary source of content for MXB107.

    • Note that each week’s posting may also include updates or revisions to the previous weeks’ materials; if so, we will tell you. In any case, you are responsible for the most recent version of the readings.

    • When you read the weekly content, treat it like attending or listening to a traditional lecture: write down questions or comments you have about the material and the examples. These can serve as a guide for your participation in the weekly consultation sessions.

  • Watch the recorded videos —

    • Each week, the teaching staff will post short videos going over the examples from the weekly notes in more detail and working through solutions to the problems. These videos will contain no content not already in the weekly notes, but will seek to clarify and expand on them, and show systematic solutions to example problems in the weekly notes.

    • As with the notes themselves, as you watch the videos write down questions or comments about the material and the examples. Again, these can serve as a guide for your participation in the weekly consultation sessions.

    • Additional recordings of workshops and consultation sessions will also be posted each week, if you cannot attend these workshops or consultations, make sure you watch these recordings.

  • Do the activities —

    • Attend the weekly consultation sessions and actively participate, these are your opportunity to interact directly with the teaching staff and gain the benefit of live feedback. Bring your questions and comments on the readings and the videos, or questions about the assessment.

    • Attempt or complete the pre-workshop activities. Part of MXB107 requires that you learn the statistical programming language R and its graphical user interface R Studio. There are materials posted on the Blackboard page to help you get started using R and R Studio, and each week there will be pre-workshop exercises for you to attempt or complete.

    • Attend the workshops. Workshops are another opportunity for your to interact with the teaching staff and your fellow students to get hands-on experience with example problems similar to the assessment and to learn and practice your R skills.

Zoom Guidelines

Much of the live online interactions will take place on Zoom; it is important to remember that you should view these experiences the same as classroom experiences, and you should expect the same level of respect and consideration for Zoom sessions as you would for the classroom. To facilitate this, here are a few guidelines:

  • When joining a Zoom session, you will be placed in a waiting room and then allowed to enter by the session host, your microphone will be muted. We request that you please keep your microphone muted unless you ask a question or otherwise participate in the session.

  • Be respectful of bandwidth. Please join the session with your camera turned off and kept off for the session. This helps make sure that everyone can benefit from the session regardless of the quality of their connection.

  • If you don’t want to speak, please post your questions or comments so that everyone can read them, the host will re-read your question or comment aloud and respond.

  • Please realise that using technology like Zoom as a part of the learning experience is still new, and there may be issues. Please be patient and know that your constructive feedback is welcome and appreciated.

And finally ...

We recognise that taking units online can seem daunting; the most important thing to keep in mind is that the teaching staff focuses on creating the best possible experience for students and helping them learn. Communication is important; if you feel you are falling behind or getting lost, speak up, you are probably not alone. If we could improve the unit and how it is offered online, please let us know. Learning is a cooperative experience and an active experience, so please communicate with the teaching staff and fellow students and participate in the unit activities throughout the semester.
